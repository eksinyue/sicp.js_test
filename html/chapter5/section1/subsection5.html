5.1.5 Instruction Summary

A controller instruction in our register-machine language has one of the following forms, where each $\textit{input}_i$ is either {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$reg($}$\textit{register-name}${\lstinline[mathescape=true]$)$} or {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$constant($}$\textit{constant-value}${\lstinline[mathescape=true]$)$}.

These instructions were introduced in section~\ref{sec:register-machine-language}: \begin{JavaScript} assign($\textit{register-name}$, reg($\textit{register-name}$)) assign($\textit{register-name}$, constant($\textit{constant-value}$)) assign($\textit{register-name}$, list(op($\textit{operation-name}$), $\textit{input}_{1}$, $\ldots$, $\textit{input}_{n}$)) perform(list(op($\textit{operation-name}$), $\textit{input}_{1}$, $\ldots$, $\textit{input}_{n}$)) test(list(op($\textit{operation-name}$), $\textit{input}_{1}$, $\ldots$, $\textit{input}_{n}$)) branch(label($\textit{label-name}$)) go_to(label($\textit{label-name}$)) \end{JavaScript}

The use of registers to hold labels was introduced in section~\ref{sec:subroutines}: \begin{JavaScript} assign($\textit{register-name}$, label($\textit{label-name}$)) go_to(reg($\textit{register-name}$)) \end{JavaScript}

Instructions to use the stack were introduced in section~\ref{sec:stack-recursion}: \begin{JavaScript} save($\textit{register-name}$) restore($\textit{register-name}$) \end{JavaScript}

The only kind of constant-value we have seen so far is a number, but later we will use strings, symbols, and lists. For example, {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$constant("abc")$} is the string {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$"abc"$}, {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$constant(list(a, b, c))$} is the list {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$list(a, b, c)$}, and {\lstinline[mathescape=true]$constant(null)$} is the empty list.
